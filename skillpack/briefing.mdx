The briefing skill transforms raw brain data into a daily intelligence briefing. It is the difference between "you have a meeting at 3" and "you have a meeting at 3 with Pedro — last time you discussed the Series B timeline, he was concerned about burn rate, here's the latest from his company page." The briefing runs every morning via cron and draws on context that accumulates over time.

## Briefing components

A complete daily briefing has six sections, each populated by specific brain queries:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Today's meetings" icon="calendar">
    Participants with dossier excerpts, shared history, and open threads. Every attendee gets a context paragraph from their brain page.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Active deals" icon="handshake">
    Pipeline status, deadlines approaching in the next 7 days, and recent timeline changes.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Time-sensitive threads" icon="clock">
    Action items due today or overdue. Follow-ups that have gone quiet. Items with deadlines in the next 48 hours.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Recent brain changes" icon="arrows-rotate">
    Pages updated in the last 24 hours: what changed and why, drawn from timeline entries.
  </Card>
  <Card title="People in play" icon="users">
    Person pages updated in the last 7 days or with high recent activity.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Stale alerts" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    Pages flagged as stale that are relevant to today's meetings — compiled truth older than the latest timeline entry.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## The briefing workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Load calendar context">
    Pull today's events. For each meeting, extract attendee names, meeting time, and any notes from the calendar entry. This is the seed list for meeting prep queries.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Meeting prep for each attendee">
    For every person on today's calendar:

    ```bash
    gbrain search "<attendee name>"         # find their brain page
    gbrain get people/<slug>                # load compiled truth + recent timeline
    gbrain graph people/<slug> --depth 2   # relationship context
    gbrain search "<name> <company>"       # recent mentions across brain
    ```

    Synthesize: role, background, shared history, open items, relationship temperature. If no brain page exists, note the gap: "No brain page for Sarah Chen — consider enrichment."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Query active deals">
    ```bash
    gbrain query "active deals status"
    gbrain list --type deal --tag active
    ```

    For each active deal: deadline, recent timeline entries, any status changes in the last 7 days.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Surface time-sensitive threads">
    ```bash
    gbrain query "pending commitments follow-ups"
    gbrain query "action items due this week"
    ```

    Extract open items with deadlines in the next 48 hours. Flag overdue follow-ups.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Recent brain changes">
    ```bash
    gbrain list --sort updated --limit 20
    gbrain query "meetings this week"
    ```

    What the agent updated in the last 24 hours and why — drawn from timeline entries.
  </Step>
  <Step title="People in play">
    ```bash
    gbrain search --type person --sort updated --limit 10
    ```

    People with recent activity: updated in last 7 days, high timeline volume, or flagged in recent meetings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Before a meeting

The meeting prep workflow in detail. Run these queries for every attendee on the calendar invite:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the brain page">
    ```bash
    gbrain search "<attendee name>"
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="Load compiled truth">
    ```bash
    gbrain get people/<slug>
    ```
    Read: Executive Summary, State, What They Believe, Relationship, open threads.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Graph for relationship context">
    ```bash
    gbrain graph people/<slug> --depth 2
    ```
    Who do they know? What companies are they connected to? Any shared connections with you?
  </Step>
  <Step title="Recent mentions">
    ```bash
    gbrain search "<name> <company>"
    ```
    Anything mentioned about them in the last few weeks across meetings, emails, or notes?
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Before an email reply

Before drafting or triaging any email, load sender context:

```bash
gbrain search "<sender name>"
gbrain get people/<slug>
```

Read compiled truth to understand who they are, what they care about, and your relationship history. This turns a cold reply into an informed one.

## Output format

The briefing is one concise paragraph per section. Actionable items are bolded.

```
DAILY BRIEFING — 2026-04-10
========================

MEETINGS TODAY
- 3:00 PM  Team Sync
  Participants: Pedro Franceschi (people/pedro-franceschi — co-founder Brex,
  last discussed Series B timeline 2026-03-15, **open thread: burn rate concern**)

ACTIVE DEALS
- Acme Corp Series A — term sheet stage, **deadline: 2026-04-15**
  Recent: Updated valuation discussed in board call 2026-04-08

ACTION ITEMS
- **Follow up with Pedro on burn rate** — due 2026-04-10
- Review Q2 board deck draft — overdue since 2026-04-07

RECENT CHANGES (24h)
- people/sarah-chen — updated State: new role as VP Engineering at Acme Corp
- meetings/2026-04-09-board-call — ingested, 4 entity pages updated

PEOPLE IN PLAY
- Pedro Franceschi — 3 timeline updates this week, meeting today
- Diana Chen — email thread re Q3 launch, no reply in 5 days
```

## Scheduling

Run the briefing each morning via cron, before quiet hours end:

```
# Daily briefing — 8:00 AM
0 8 * * * "Generate my daily briefing: load today's calendar, prep for
  each meeting using gbrain (search attendees, get pages, graph depth 2),
  pull active deals, surface time-sensitive threads and overdue items,
  summarize recent brain changes. Format with one paragraph per section,
  bold all action items."
  --name "morning-briefing"
```

<Note>
  The briefing respects travel-aware timezone handling. The agent reads your calendar for flights and out-of-office blocks to infer your current location. All times are shown in your local timezone, not UTC or your home timezone.
</Note>

## Integration with the EA pattern

The briefing is the daily manifestation of the executive assistant pattern. The brain transforms basic EA work into contextual EA work:

| Without GBrain | With GBrain |
|---------------|-------------|
| "You have a meeting at 3" | "You have a meeting at 3 with Pedro — last time: Series B timeline, burn rate concern" |
| "Email from Sarah" | "Email from Sarah — VP Engineering at Acme, open thread about Q3 launch deadline" |
| "Jordan wants to meet" | "Jordan — you haven't met in 6 weeks, she has an open thread about the Q3 launch" |

The scheduling context also benefits: "You haven't met with Diana in 6 weeks and she has an open thread about the Q3 launch" is a useful scheduling nudge, surfaced automatically from the brain.

## Tools used

- `query` — semantic search for active deals, pending commitments, meeting context
- `get_page` — load compiled truth for meeting attendees
- `list_pages` — list pages by type, sorted by update time
- `get_health` — stale alerts relevant to today's meetings
- `get_timeline` — recent timeline entries for specific pages
