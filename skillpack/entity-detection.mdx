Entity detection is the mechanism that makes the brain compound. Spawn a lightweight sub-agent on every inbound message — no exceptions unless the message is purely operational. The sub-agent captures two categories of signal with equal priority: original thinking from the user, and named entity mentions. Missing either one creates a gap in the brain.

## Two signal types

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Original thinking (PRIMARY)" icon="lightbulb">
    Ideas, observations, theses, frameworks, and philosophical riffs. This is the highest-value signal in the entire system — it compounds into essays, talks, and strategic insight.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Named entities (SECONDARY)" icon="people-group">
    People, companies, organizations, and products. Each becomes a brain page that accumulates context over time.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Warning>
  Both signal types have equal priority and must be captured. An agent that captures entity mentions but ignores original thinking is losing the highest-value content in the system.
</Warning>

## Capturing original thinking

The user's original observations, theses, frameworks, and hot takes. This is the highest-value content in the entire brain.

**Capture the user's exact phrasing.** The language is the insight. "The ambition-to-lifespan ratio has never been more broken" captures something that "tension between ambition and mortality" doesn't. Don't clean it up. Don't paraphrase.

Route by authorship:

| Signal | Destination |
|--------|-------------|
| User generated the idea | `brain/originals/{slug}.md` |
| World concept they reference | `brain/concepts/{slug}.md` |
| Product or business idea | `brain/ideas/{slug}.md` |
| Personal reflection or pattern | `brain/personal/reflections/` |

**What counts:** Original observations about how the world works, novel connections between disparate things, frameworks and mental models, pattern recognition moments, hot takes with reasoning, metaphors that reveal new angles.

**What doesn't count:** Routine operational messages ("ok", "do it"), pure questions without embedded observations, echoing back something the agent said.

**Naming rule:** Use the user's own language for the slug. `meatsuit-maintenance-tax` not `biological-needs-maintenance-overhead`. The vividness is the concept.

<Tip>
  Cross-link originals to people who shaped the thinking, companies where it played out, meetings where it was discussed, and other originals it connects to. An original without cross-links is a dead original. The connections are the intelligence.
</Tip>

## Capturing entity mentions

For each person, company, or organization mentioned:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Search the brain">
    Run `gbrain search "name"` — does a page already exist?
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assess and route">
    - **Rich page exists:** load it silently for context. Use the compiled truth in your response.
    - **Thin page exists:** spawn background enrichment.
    - **No page, entity is notable:** create a page with `gbrain put <type/slug>`, then enrich.
    - **No page, passing mention:** note but don't create. Not every name warrants a page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update with new facts">
    For new information about an existing entity, append to their timeline. Never rewrite timeline entries — they are the evidence trail.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Back-link from the source">
    The iron law: back-link FROM entity pages TO the source that mentions them. Format:
    `- **YYYY-MM-DD** | Referenced in [page title](path/to/page.md) — context`
  </Step>
</Steps>

### What counts as notable

- People the user interacts with or discusses (not random mentions)
- Companies relevant to the user's work, investments, or interests
- Concepts or frameworks the user references or creates
- The user's own original thinking (ideas, theses, observations) — highest value

## The detection protocol

```
Every inbound message
  |
  v
Is this purely operational? (e.g., "ok", "do it", "yes")
  | YES: skip entity detection
  | NO: continue
  v
Spawn sub-agent (fast, cheap model — e.g. Claude Haiku)
  |
  v
Scan for original thinking
  | Found: capture verbatim in originals/{slug}.md
  |        cross-link to related entities
  v
Scan for entity mentions (people, companies)
  | For each entity:
  |   gbrain search "name"
  |   → exists + rich: load for context
  |   → exists + thin: enrich in background
  |   → doesn't exist + notable: create + enrich
  |   → doesn't exist + passing: skip
  v
Back-link all created/updated pages to source
  |
  v
Sync: git commit + gbrain sync --no-pull --no-embed
```

<Note>
  Don't block the conversation. Entity detection and enrichment should happen alongside the response, not before it. The user shouldn't wait for brain writes to get an answer. Spawn and forget.
</Note>

## The iron law of back-linking

When a person or company with a brain page is mentioned in any brain file, that file must be linked **from** the person or company's brain page. This is the connective tissue of the brain. An unlinked mention is a broken brain.

Format: append to the entity's Timeline or See Also section:

```markdown
- **2026-04-07** | Referenced in [Team Sync meeting](meetings/2026-04-07-team-sync.md) — discussed Series B timeline
```

Every skill that writes to the brain enforces this rule. There are no exceptions.

## Practical example

A user mentions "Pedro" in conversation:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Search">
    ```bash
    gbrain search "Pedro"
    ```
    Returns: `people/pedro-franceschi` — rich page exists.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Load context">
    ```bash
    gbrain get people/pedro-franceschi
    ```
    Agent reads compiled truth: co-founder of Brex, Series B concerns, last met 2026-03-15.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Respond with context">
    Agent's response references the relationship history, open threads, and latest timeline entries. The user gets a better answer than they would from a cold response.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update if new info">
    If the conversation reveals new facts about Pedro, append to his timeline immediately:
    ```markdown
    - **2026-04-10** | Mentioned concern about Q2 burn rate. [Source: User, direct message, 2026-04-10 2:33 PM PT]
    ```
  </Step>
</Steps>

## The originals folder

Most knowledge systems capture what you found — articles, meetings, people. The originals folder captures what you think. Over time, it becomes a searchable archive of the user's intellectual output, organized by topic.

This is the memex at its most powerful: not just remembering what you read, but remembering what you thought about what you read.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Authorship test — which folder?">
    - User generated the idea? → `originals/{slug}.md`
    - User's unique synthesis of someone else's ideas? → `originals/` (the synthesis is original)
    - World concept someone else coined? → `concepts/{slug}.md`
    - Product or business idea? → `ideas/{slug}.md`
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Cross-linking originals">
    Every original should link to:
    - People who shaped the thinking
    - Companies where it played out
    - Meetings where it was discussed
    - Books and media that influenced it
    - Other originals it connects to (ideas form clusters)

    An original without cross-links is a dead original. The connections are the intelligence.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
